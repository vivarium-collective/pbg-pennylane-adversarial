INCONCLUSIVE
Real-test node (instantaneous_growth_rate) verdict: INCONCLUSIVE. Sanity-check node cluster (near-deterministic mass/replication arithmetic) verdict: STOP. See §2-3 below for why these are reported separately rather than one blended number.

1. Is the harness itself trustworthy at N=4? (data-sufficiency calibration)

Before trusting any real-data classical-baseline number, this ran the identical harness (z-score fit on training folds, DMD, SINDy, leave-one-trajectory-out evaluation) on a correctly-specified linear synthetic system (ported idea from pbg-pennylane-data-reuploading's NonlinearProcessGenerator -- see dataset_transform/synthetic_dynamics.py), sweeping trajectory count. Unlike that generator's own audited flaw (an asserted, unvalidated "Bayes-optimal accuracy"), the achievable R2 ceiling here is measured directly from the same generating code path.

Empirical finding, not assumed going in: at the real N=4, DMD's median LOTO R2 is -1.257 — worse than predicting the mean — on a system with a measured achievable ceiling of 1.000. An unconstrained 8x8 operator has too many free parameters for 3 training trajectories to pin down, and a held-out 4th trajectory occupies a different, unvisited region of state space. SINDy's sparsity partially compensates (median R2 0.310) but still falls well short of the ceiling. Both improve steadily past N=8. Conclusion: any real-data DMD score at N=4 should be discounted almost entirely; any real-data SINDy score should be read as an underestimate of true recoverable signal.

2. Real-data result: sanity-check nodes vs. the one real test

todo.md phase 5 pre-registered a split: cell_mass, dry_mass, protein_mass, rna_mass, dna_mass, volume, and number_of_oric are close to deterministic simulator arithmetic (mass components sum; dna_mass tracks number_of_oric almost exactly) — recovering them is an expected sanity check, not a finding. instantaneous_growth_rate is the one genuinely emergent relationship (Scott et al. growth-law territory). Blending both into one aggregate median would let the trivial cluster's high score hide a total failure on the node that actually matters, so they're kept separate below.

Sanity-check cluster: DMD/SINDy reach median R2 0.913 vs. persistence 0.376 — recovered about as well as expected for near-algebraic identities. Real-test node: best classical arm (DMD) reaches median R2 -0.325 vs. persistence -5.316 — no arm beats its own mean prediction. Per §1's calibration, this cannot be read as "no learnable growth-rate dynamics" on its own -- it is exactly the outcome this harness would also produce on a correctly-specified linear system at this N. Concrete next step: more real trajectories and/or flux-level features beyond state snapshots, before concluding growth_rate has no learnable one-step dynamics.

Outlier fold flagged: holdout trajectory 3 causes catastrophic failure (double-digit-negative R2) even on the sanity-check cluster, unlike the other 3 folds. Worth checking whether that trajectory's lineage_seed/generation combination reflects a genuinely different physiological regime (it is the longest, latest-generation trajectory pulled) or a data-quality issue, before treating all 4 folds as equally representative.

3. DMD eigenvalue spectrum & SINDy term recovery

Eigenvalue magnitudes near 1 indicate slow/persistent modes -- a quantitative version of the growth-rate-variance check from phase 1. rank_used=7 of 8 singular values retained for this fold (singular_values= [15.71, 5.89, 2.4, 0.69, 0.38, 0.26, 0.0, 0.0]).

SINDy coefficient caveat: the largest recovered coefficient magnitude across all folds is 2253.5 on z-scored (unit-variance) inputs -- far larger than a well-conditioned sparse fit should need. This is the same severe collinearity phase 1 already found among the mass-group features (near-exact linear dependencies like cell_mass ~= protein_mass + rna_mass + dna_mass + ...), showing up here as large, canceling coefficients rather than a numerically stable sparse structure. Read SINDy's recovered terms as evidence of which nodes are involved, not as physically meaningful coefficient values.

4. Decision-gate verdict (full text)

SANITY-CHECK nodes (cell_mass, dry_mass, protein_mass, rna_mass, dna_mass, volume, number_of_oric): DMD/SINDy median R2=0.913 vs. persistence 0.376 -- near-deterministic mass/replication arithmetic, already well explained by a linear fit as expected; verdict 'STOP' (not worth QGRNN/GNN investment on these specifically). REAL-TEST node (instantaneous_growth_rate): best classical arm (DMD) median R2=-0.325 vs. persistence -5.316 -- no arm beats its own mean prediction on the one genuinely emergent relationship. Per the calibration finding, this N=4-driven weakness could reflect real absence of learnable structure OR simply insufficient data/features to see it -- cannot distinguish the two here, so verdict is 'INCONCLUSIVE', not a flat STOP: pulling more real trajectories and/or richer flux-level features (not just state snapshots) is the concrete next step before concluding growth_rate has no learnable one-step dynamics. Calibration context: at N=4 on a correctly-specified linear synthetic system, this harness's DMD arm reaches median R2=-1.257 (SINDy 0.310) against a measured achievable ceiling of 1.000 -- both well short of the ceiling due to sample count alone, not a real-data property. DMD's median R2 doesn't clear 0.4 until N=12 trajectories in this sweep. Also flagged: holdout trajectory(ies) [3] caused catastrophic failure even on the sanity-check cluster (R2 as low as double-digit negative) -- worth checking whether that trajectory (lineage_seed/generation combination) reflects a genuinely different physiological regime or a data-quality issue before trusting the aggregate across all 4 folds equally.
